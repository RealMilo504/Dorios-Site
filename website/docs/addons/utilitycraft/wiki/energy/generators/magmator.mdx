# Magmator

The **Magmator** is a powerful energy generator in **UtilityCraft** designed for mid-to-late game progression.  
It burns **lava** as fuel to produce **Dorios Energy (DE)**, offering one of the fastest energy generation rates available but requiring more maintenance than other generators.

---

## Energy Production & Storage Capacity

The Magmator converts lava into DE at very high speeds.  
Each **Lava Bucket** provides **100,000 DE**, and higher tiers increase both energy generation rate and storage capacity.

| Tier | Rate (DE/t) | Energy Capacity (DE) | Lava Storage (mB) |
|------|--------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Basic | 50 DE/t | 80k DE | 8,000 mB |
| Advanced | 200 DE/t | 320k DE | 32,000 mB |
| Expert | 800 DE/t | 1.28M DE | 128,000 mB |
| Ultimate | 5,000 DE/t | 8M DE | 800,000 mB |

Each tier increases the conversion speed and efficiency, allowing the Magmator to power larger setups efficiently.

---

## Feeding Lava to the Magmator

The Magmator requires lava to operate.  
There are two main ways to provide fuel:

### 1. Manual Interaction

Players can manually insert **Lava Buckets** or **Lava Balls** directly into the Magmator.  
This method is simple and effective for early-game testing or small setups.

### 2. Fluid Transport Systems

For automated fueling, connect the Magmator to **Fluid Pumps** or **Fluid Extractors** using **Fluid Pipes**.

**Fluid Pump:**  
Fast and ideal for high-demand setups. It can extract lava from **Fluid Tanks**, **Crucibles**, or natural **lava source blocks**.

**Fluid Extractors:**  
Slightly slower but more flexible. Multiple extractors can pull from different sources, making them useful for modular or compact systems.

The interface includes:
- **Fluid Tank Display** – shows the amount of stored lava.  
- **Energy Bar** – displays the current DE level.  
- **Progress Bar** – indicates how much of the current lava batch has been consumed.  
- **Output Flow** – transfers stored energy to nearby machines or batteries automatically.
