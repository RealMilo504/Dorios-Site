# Thermo Generator

The **Thermo Generator** produces **Dorios Energy (DE)** when placed above a heat source such as **lava**, **magma blocks**, or **fire**.  
The hotter the block, the more energy it generates. However, it requires **water for cooling** to function properly.

---

## Energy & Storage Capacities

| Tier | Rate (DE/t) | Energy Capacity | Water Capacity | Water Usage |
|------|--------------|----------------|----------------|--------------|
| **Basic** | 20 DE/t | 32,000 DE | 2,000 mB | 0.5 mB/t |
| **Advanced** | 80 DE/t | 128,000 DE | 8,000 mB | 2 mB/t |
| **Expert** | 320 DE/t | 512,000 DE | 32,000 mB | 8 mB/t |
| **Ultimate** | 2,000 DE/t | 3,200,000 DE | 200,000 mB | 50 mB/t |

## Feeding Water to the Thermo Generator

The Thermo Generator consumes **water** to prevent overheating and maintain stable operation.

### Manual Interaction
Use a **Water Bucket** to manually fill the generator — ideal for quick setups or testing.

### Fluid Transport Systems
You can automate water input using **Fluid Pumps** or **Fluid Extractors** connected with **Fluid Pipes**.

- **Fluid Pump:** The fastest and most efficient method.  
  Can draw water from a **Sink**, **Fluid Tank**, or even **source blocks**.  
- **Fluid Extractors:** Slower but more flexible — multiple units can supply water steadily.
